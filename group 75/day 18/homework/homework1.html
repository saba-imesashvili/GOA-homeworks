In Georgian cuisine, khinkali (Georgian: ხინკალი, romanized: khink'ali) is a kind of dumpling made of twisted knobs of dough, stuffed with spiced meat or vegetables.[1][2] Khinkali originate in the mountainous regions of eastern Georgia. The invention of the dish is credited to the Mokheves, as well as the Pshavs, Mtiuls, and Khevsurs. Traditionally, khinkali are made with coarsely chopped meat. In the mountain areas, this method of preparation is still used
